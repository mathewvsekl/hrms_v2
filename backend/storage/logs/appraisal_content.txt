   Avantgarde Annual Appraisal - 2025  
 
General Overview of the  Annual Appraisal Form  and what to do in each section  
This Annual Appraisal  Form  is designed to capture an employee’s performance during the appraisal year, based 
on their key deliverables, measurable outcomes, and development needs. The Annua  Appraisal F orm ensures a 
structured, fair, and transparent evaluation process by combining self -assessment, manager assessment, and 
a formal discussion, followed by calibration and leadership review.

Section B: Employee Self -Assessment – Key Result Areas (KRAs) and Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)  
Key Result Areas (KRAs) represent the broad responsibility areas of your role, while Key Performance Indicators 
(KPIs) are the specific, measurable objectives used to assess performance within each KRA during the appraisal 
year. Together, KRAs and KPIs ref lect the key outcomes, deliverables, and responsibilities expected from your 
position and are aligned with your department’s objectives, such as Sales, Tenders, Operations, Finance, 
Administration, or HR.  
Employees are required to list a minimum of five (5) KPIs for the year, clearly stating the relevant KRA and KPI for 
each entry. For every KPI, employees should summarize the key achievements or results delivered during the 
year. Wherever possible, this sh ould include measurable outcomes, milestones achieved, or specific 
contributions that demonstrate performance.  
As part of Section B, employees are also required to provide a self -rating on a scale of 1 to 5 for each listed KPI, 
based on their overall performance against that indicator.  
The Department Head / Reporting Manager will review the employee’s inputs, provide comments where required, 
and assign their ratings for each KPI during the assessment stage.  
 

 
Guidelines – Soft Skills Assessment (Mandatory KPI)  
In addition to role -specific KPIs, one KPI related to professional behaviour and soft skills is mandatory for all 
employees.  These soft skills  will be assessed separately using the dedicated soft skills rating table  provided, 
where employees are required to rate themselves against each listed soft skill using the prescribed rating scale.  
Rating Scale (1 –10) for Sof t Skills:  
Employees are required to provide a self -rating for each listed soft skill on a scale of 1 to 10, where , where 
10 being  the highest level, reflecting consistent demonstration of the skill at an exceptional and impactful 
standard, and 1 being  the lowest level, indicating the skill is not meeting expectations and requires 
significant improvement.  
The Department Head / Reporting Manager will review the employee’s self -ratings and provide their own rating 
(1–10) for each soft skill based on observations and discussion.  
Section C: Employee Summary and Development Plan  
This section is to be completed by the employee.  
Employees are requested to reflect on their overall performance during the appraisal year and provide a 
summary  of their key contributions and achievements. They should also identify areas where improvement, 
learning, or further development is required.  
The objective of this section is to encourage meaningful self -reflection and to help identify development needs, 
or support that may enhance the employee’s effectiveness and performance in the coming year.  
Section D: Manager Assessment – Overall Performance Evaluation  
This section should be completed by the reporting manager based on the employee’s overall performance during 
the appraisal year. Managers are requested to review the employee’s self -assessment provided in Section B and 
provide a fair and balanced evaluatio n. They have to  summarize thei r observations and give a final overall rating 
(1–5) in accordance with the Rating Scale Reference Table for KPI’s.  
Section E: HR Review & Finalization (HR Use Only)  
This section is to be completed by the HR after reviewing the employee’s appraisal form, manager assessment, 
and post -calibration outcomes.  
The objective of this section is to consolidate ratings, record calibration adjustments (if any), and finalize the 
employee’s performance rating for internal records, increment, and annual bonus decisions.

How to Use the Annual Apprais al Form  
Please read the guidelines below to understand which sections must be completed by the employee and which 
must be completed by the Department Head / Reporting Manager.  
1. Sections to be Completed by the Employee  
Employees must complete the following sections before submitting the form to their Department Head / 
Reporting Manager:  
✓ Section A – Employee Details  
 
• Fill in all personal and role -related information accurately.  
 
✓ Section B – Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)  
 • List a minimum of five (5) KPIs relevant to your role for the appraisal year.  
 • For each KPI:  
o Mention the Key Result Area / KPI  
o Summarize the key achievements or results delivered during the year.  
o Provide a self -rating (1–5) for each role -specific KPI.  
 
Mandatory Soft Skills KPI  
• In addition to role -specific KPIs, one mandatory KPI related to soft skills applies to all employees.  
• This soft skills KPI is assessed through a separate Soft Skills Rating Scale  provided under Section B.  
• Employees are required to:  
o Provide a self -rating on a scale of 1 –10 for each listed soft skill.  
o The combined soft skills scores will be:  
o Converted into an overall Soft Skills Percentage . 
 
✓ Section C – Employee Summary & Development Plan  
• Provide an overall summary of your performance during the year.  
• Reflect on challenges faced, areas for improvement, learning needs, and any training, mentoring, or 
support required to enhance performance in the coming year.  
After completing Sections A –C, employees must submit the form to their Department Head / Reporting 
Manager . 
2. Sections to be Completed by the Department Head / Reporting Manager  
After reviewing the employee’s entries, the Department Head / Reporting Manager must complete the following:  
✓ In Section B — Department Head Rating  
 
• Review the employee’s role -specific KPIs, key achievements/results, and self -ratings.  
• Review the employee’s soft skills self -ratings provided in the Soft Skills Rating Table.  
• Conduct a performance discussion with the employee (in person or online).  
For each role -specific KPI, the Department Head / Reporting Manager must:  
• Provide comments on performance, outcomes, and effectiveness.  
• Assign a KPI rating (1 –5) in the designated column.  
For soft skills assessment, the Department Head / Reporting Manager must:  
• Provide ratings (1 –10) for each listed soft skill in the Soft Skills Rating table . 
 
✓ Section D — Manager Assessment (Manager Use Only)  
 
• Provide an overall assessment of the employee’s performance.  
• Assign the Manager’s Final Overall KPI Rating (1 –5). 
• Record recommendations related to performance and readiness for future responsibilities.  
• This section contains confidential evaluation details and must not be shared with the employee.  
4. Section to be Completed by HR  
✓ Section E – HR Review & Finalization (HR Use Only)  
This section is reserved for HR to review, validate, and finalize the appraisal outcomes. HR will:  
• Review the Soft Skills KPI Rating (post internal mapping).  
• Review the Overall KPI Rating provided by the Department Head / Reporting Manager.  
• Apply calibration adjustments, if required, to ensure consistency and fairness across teams.  
• Record the Revised Overall KPI Rating (post calibration).  
• Document HR observations or remarks, if any.  
• Confirm the Final Performance Rating for internal records.  
• Confirm eligibility for salary increment and annual bonus, as applicable.  
This section is confidential and intended strictly for internal HR and leadership review .
